# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Owners and decision-makers of small, credibility-sensitive businesses in Portugal who are evaluating whether to commission a custom website.

Two segments, formal first:

1. **Primary — formal service businesses.** Lawyers, clinics, consultants, institutional SMEs. They arrive skeptical, often after a bad template experience or a quote from a cheap builder. Their job: judge, in the first ten seconds, whether this studio can make them look credible to *their* clients. They read carefully, care about trust signals (clear process, clear price, fast reply), and will not tolerate anything that feels generic or amateur.
2. **Secondary — creators and identity-led small businesses.** Shops, artisans, event producers (the current portfolio: Ludy Artes, Cão na Rua, Gentle Laughter). They want a site that captures a distinctive personality, not a corporate template. They convert on craft and on seeing real work that "sounds like" its owner.

Context of use: mostly a solo browse on a phone or laptop, deciding whether to send an inquiry. The site is the entire sales pitch before any conversation. The single desired outcome is a qualified inquiry (form, WhatsApp, or email).

## Product Purpose

Barata Studio is the one-person web studio of Sérgio (Berto) Barata, selling custom, hand-built websites under the line "Do briefing ao launch: web feita à mão." The homepage exists to convert a skeptical visitor into a qualified inquiry by *being* the proof: the site itself has to be the best argument that this studio makes credible, clear, non-generic websites.

Success looks like: the visitor believes the studio is more considered than a template shop, understands the process and price range without friction, and reaches out. The site must also quietly disqualify template-shoppers looking for the cheapest option, so inquiries arrive pre-aligned on value.

## Brand Personality

**Calm. Handcrafted. Credible.**

Voice: quiet confidence, no hype, no urgency theater. Plain Portuguese, honest about scope and price. The brand speaks like a careful maker who has nothing to prove and therefore does not shout. Warmth comes from the handmade wordmark and human copy; seriousness comes from restraint, generous space, and a single disciplined accent. The emotional goal is trust and a sense of craft: "this person will treat my project with care."

## Anti-references

- **SaaS / startup-template landing pages.** No hero-metric slabs as filler, no identical icon-in-a-circle card grids, no "modern gradient" marketing look.
- **"Startup AI" aesthetics.** No neon purples or blues, no glow-on-black, no synthetic tech-demo vibe.
- **Generic glassmorphism.** No decorative frosted-glass everywhere. Any translucency is a deliberate material (smoked glass on process panels), never a default.
- **Cheap-builder look.** Nothing that reads as Wix/one-click template: no pure `#000`/`#fff`, no stock-photo hero, no clip-art icons.
- **Overproduced motion.** No bounce, no elastic, no objects flying around, no particle effects. If it feels like a demo reel, it is wrong.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the proof.** Practice what you preach. Every pixel is a live sample of the service being sold. If a section is not something we would ship to a paying client, it does not ship here.
2. **Quiet confidence over persuasion.** Sell by restraint, not by pressure. No countdowns, no exclamation urgency. Space, rhythm, and craft do the convincing.
3. **Show real work, not claims.** Proof is concrete and labeled (real projects, real metrics like "Performance Google 100/100", real client quotes). Never invent generic trust badges.
4. **Clarity before charm.** Make process, scope, price, and next step legible with zero friction (clear pricing, "resposta em menos de 24h"). Charm never costs the visitor clarity.
5. **One voice, held back.** A single petróleo accent, a single handmade type voice. Rarity is the luxury. When in doubt, remove, don't add.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA** as a non-negotiable baseline (achieved this cycle: secondary text ≥4.5:1 on both dark and warm surfaces, visible `:focus-visible` rings, semantic heading outline, skip link). Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` by disabling the scroll choreography. Touch targets ≥44×44 on mobile. Keep the hand-drawn display font for headings and buttons only; never for long body copy, so readability is never traded for personality.
